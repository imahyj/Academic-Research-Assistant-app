<div class="section">
<span class="section-header">Main Feature — Climate Change & Urban Heat Islands</span>
<div class="two-col">

### What is an Urban Heat Island?
Urban heat islands (UHIs) occur where built environments raise local temperatures relative to surrounding rural areas (Zhao et al., 2014). UHIs are heterogeneous and often overlap with vulnerable neighborhoods.

### How climate change amplifies urban heat
Anthropogenic warming raises baseline temperatures and increases frequency of extreme heat. Attribution studies show a measurable share of heat-related mortality is already due to human-induced warming (Vicedo‑Cabrera et al., 2021).

### Mechanisms & evidence-based interventions
- Surface materials and geometry trap heat.
- Vegetation and evapotranspiration offer cooling when water and species are appropriate.
- Hybrid interventions (green + albedo) offer the largest local cooling at human scales (Li et al., 2014).

</div>
</div>

<div class="section">
<span class="section-header">Practical Applications</span>
- **Assess**: Map UHI hotspots and vulnerabilities.
- **Cool**: Deploy cool roofs, cool pavements, and targeted street trees.
- **Target**: Focus on equity — subsidize interventions in heat-vulnerable neighborhoods.
</div>

<div class="section">
<span class="section-header">Key Takeaways</span>
<div class="keytake">
- UHIs worsen heat exposure and are amplified by climate change (Vicedo‑Cabrera et al., 2021).  
- Local climate controls the UHI response and intervention effectiveness (Zhao et al., 2014).  
- Combined strategies provide greatest benefit; pair greening with high‑albedo materials (Li et al., 2014).
</div>
</div>
